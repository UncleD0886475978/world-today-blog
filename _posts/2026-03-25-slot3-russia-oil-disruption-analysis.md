**Analysis — March 25, 2026**

For years, analysts warned Ukraine's greatest long-term weapon against Russia was not on the battlefield but in the energy infrastructure funding Moscow's war machine. This week, that weapon was fired with unprecedented precision.

## The Scale of the Disruption

Approximately 40% of Russia's oil export capacity has been taken offline through Ukrainian drone strikes on export terminals, pipeline damage, and tanker seizures. The Novatek Ust-Luga terminal was struck twice in three days. Russia's oil revenues fund 30-40% of the federal budget — a sustained disruption at this scale would inflict economic damage no sanctions regime has yet achieved.

## The Shadow Fleet Under Threat

Russia built an elaborate shadow fleet of tankers to circumvent oil price caps. That fleet is now under direct threat. The Arctic Metagaz, a Russian shadow fleet tanker, was struck by a drone, caught fire, and sank while sailing between Malta and Libya.

## A Historical Parallel

The economic pressure echoes the final years of the Soviet Union, when collapsing oil prices gutted the state's ability to sustain military spending. Ukraine is not relying on market forces this time — it is directly targeting the infrastructure that funds the war.

The energy strikes represent a new chapter — one where the front line extends 1,500 kilometres, all the way to Russia's Baltic export terminals.
