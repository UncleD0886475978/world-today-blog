**Washington / Jerusalem — March 26, 2026**

A ceasefire in the US-Iran war could be announced as early as Saturday, according to Israeli officials cited by broadcaster KAN — a stunning reversal from an administration that weeks ago demanded Tehran's unconditional surrender.

## The Political Arithmetic

A Pew Research survey found 59% of Americans believe the US made the wrong decision using military force against Iran. Just 25% say the military action is going well. Trump's approval rating on handling the conflict stands at 37%.

A Quinnipiac poll reinforces the picture: registered voters oppose military action 54% to 39%. While 86% of Republican voters remain supportive, 64% of independent voters oppose the war — spelling serious electoral danger.

## What a Concession Might Look Like

Israeli officials say Trump might halt fighting if Iran presents a substantial concession — commitments to halt uranium enrichment above certain levels, agree to international inspection access, or provide guarantees on the Strait of Hormuz.

If announced Saturday, this would be one of the most abrupt reversals in recent American military history — a war launched with rhetoric of unconditional victory, paused within a month amid polling collapse and diplomatic manoeuvring.
