**Washington / Tehran — March 25, 2026**

The United States and Iran are caught in a diplomatic paradox. President Trump declared the two sides have held productive conversations, while Iran's Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi dismissed the characterisation entirely, calling Washington's willingness to negotiate an acknowledgement of its own failure.

## A War Built on Contradictions

The conflict, now in its 26th day, has reshaped the Middle East. US and Israeli airstrikes have targeted Iranian military infrastructure and nuclear facilities, with Iran's Red Crescent reporting damage to more than 85,000 civilian locations. UN Secretary-General António Guterres declared the conflict "out of control."

For weeks the Trump administration pressed Tehran toward unconditional surrender. Now, with 59% of Americans saying the US made the wrong decision, Washington's tone has shifted sharply toward negotiation.

## Pakistan Steps Forward

Pakistan made a remarkable public offer to host ceasefire talks. Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif announced Islamabad "stands ready and honoured" to facilitate dialogue. Israeli officials told broadcaster KAN that Trump may announce a ceasefire by Saturday if Iran presents a substantial concession.

## The Economic Toll

Approximately 30% of the world's fertiliser raw materials transit the Strait of Hormuz, which Iran has threatened to restrict. Food prices have climbed globally as supply chains fracture. Oil prices remain elevated despite Washington easing sanctions on Russian and Venezuelan crude.

The next 48 hours may define the Middle East for the next decade.
