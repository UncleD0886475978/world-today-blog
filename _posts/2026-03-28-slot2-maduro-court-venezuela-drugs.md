**New York — March 28, 2026**

Nicolás Maduro, Venezuela's deposed president, and his wife Cilia Flores appeared in a Manhattan federal courtroom in beige jail uniforms this week — a striking symbol of how far the man who ruled Venezuela for a decade has fallen since his capture by US forces.

## The Charges

Maduro and Flores face drug trafficking charges in the Southern District of New York, alleging a conspiracy to flood the US with cocaine using the Venezuelan state as the distribution mechanism. Prosecutors allege Maduro personally oversaw arrangements with the FARC and other narco-trafficking organisations, turning Venezuelan military and government infrastructure into a narco-state apparatus.

## The Legal Battle

The couple's legal team pressed Judge Alvin Hellerstein to dismiss the charges entirely on jurisdictional and political grounds. The judge was unambiguous: dismissal is not happening. The case proceeds.

However, Judge Hellerstein raised a separate question: whether the federal government should continue blocking Venezuelan state funds from covering the couple's legal costs. With Venezuelan assets frozen, Maduro's lawyers argue this effectively denies adequate legal representation — a constitutional concern the judge appeared to take seriously.

## Venezuela's Painful Transition

Venezuela itself faces the challenge of rebuilding democratic institutions after years of authoritarian rule, economic collapse, and mass emigration — more than seven million Venezuelans fled abroad. The Trump administration has framed Venezuela's transition as a model for its broader approach to authoritarian regimes in the hemisphere.

The trial, expected to run for months, will produce detailed testimony about the inner workings of a narco-state.
