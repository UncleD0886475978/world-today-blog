**Washington — March 26, 2026**

President Trump announced his third postponement of the Iran deadline Thursday evening, extending to April 6. "Talks are ongoing and, despite erroneous statements to the contrary by the Fake News Media, they are going very well," he posted on Truth Social.

## The Tanker Signal

The extension came after Trump claimed during a Cabinet meeting that Iran signalled willingness to negotiate by allowing 10 Pakistani-flagged tankers through the Strait. If accurate, Tehran is using the strait as a bargaining chip — something to offer incrementally in exchange for concessions.

## Strategic Confusion at the Cabinet Table

The same meeting revealed significant uncertainty about Washington's endgame. Officials offered few concrete details about what success looks like, highlighting a dual-track strategy — peace talks plus military pressure — with no clarity on which track takes precedence. European G7 ministers gathering in France described the mood as one of deep unease, questioning whether the US has a coherent strategy.

## Democrats Delay War Powers Vote

In Congress, House Democratic leaders delayed an Iran War Powers Resolution vote until at least mid-April. Reports suggested the delay is deliberate — some Democrats privately believe the war hurts Trump politically and should be allowed to continue. The House has still not voted on the war's legality. That silence represents a significant failure of congressional oversight.
