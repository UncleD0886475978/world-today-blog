**Beirut / United Nations — March 26, 2026**

Since Israel launched its expanded offensive on March 2, the Lebanese Health Ministry reports 1,094 people killed and more than one million displaced — among the deadliest episodes in Lebanon's modern history.

## Lebanon: The Third Front

Lebanon has become the war's third front after Iran and Gaza. Hezbollah began firing rockets at northern Israel in solidarity with Tehran following months of Israeli strikes. Israel responded with an offensive Defence Minister Israel Katz compared to the destruction model used in Gaza, announcing acceleration of strikes on border villages.

Human Rights Watch stated the deliberate displacement policy constitutes war crimes. French President Macron and European Council President Costa issued strong condemnations.

## Strike Without Warning

Israeli forces struck a residential building southeast of Beirut with no prior warning — unlike previous strikes which gave civilians evacuation time. Footage showed flames engulfing the building as residents scrambled to escape.

## The UN Alarm

UN Secretary-General Guterres declared the overall conflict "out of control," warning of a far broader confrontation. A war that began between the US, Israel, and Iran has now drawn in Lebanon, threatened the Strait of Hormuz, impacted global food prices through fertiliser supply disruptions, and displaced millions across the region.

The world's most volatile region has never been more dangerous.
