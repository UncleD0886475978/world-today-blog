**Washington — March 26, 2026**

Even as Trump extended his diplomatic deadline, the Pentagon was preparing the opposite. Axios reported Thursday that military planners are developing options for a "final blow" against Iran — involving US ground forces, sustained massive bombing, and the seizure of strategic islands along the Strait of Hormuz.

## The Islands Option

Among options under consideration: a US-led invasion of Iran's strategic islands — Kharg, Larak, Abu Musa — combined with a naval blockade of ships carrying Iranian oil east of the strait. Controlling these islands would give Washington physical control over the waterway Iran has used to hold the global economy hostage. The operation would require significant amphibious capability and would almost certainly produce substantial US casualties.

## The 82nd Airborne Deploys

Two thousand soldiers from the 82nd Airborne — America's rapid-deployment force — have received Middle East deployment orders. The Pentagon is also considering 10,000 additional troops. Iran, for its part, is reportedly mobilising more than one million troops for potential ground battles.

## Americans Already Hit

Iranian forces struck Prince Sultan Air Base in Saudi Arabia, injuring 10 US service members and damaging a KC-135 refueller tanker — the first confirmed aircraft damage on the ground. Iran's IRGC also struck US-linked sites in Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, and Bahrain for the fourth consecutive day.

## The Dual-Track Danger

Pursuing diplomacy and escalation planning simultaneously creates dangerous ambiguity. Iran cannot know whether April 6 represents a genuine pause or a countdown to a final blow. Which outcome emerges depends on signals transmitted through Pakistan in the coming days.
