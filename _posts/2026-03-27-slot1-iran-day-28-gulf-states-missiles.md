**Abu Dhabi / Riyadh — March 27, 2026**

The war between the United States, Israel, and Iran has become a Gulf-wide conflict. On Day 28, Iranian forces struck multiple Gulf states in a coordinated campaign targeting energy infrastructure, military bases, and maritime routes simultaneously.

## The UAE Under Fire

The UAE intercepted 15 ballistic missiles and 11 drones. Despite the intercepts, debris killed two people in Abu Dhabi — the first civilian deaths in the UAE since the conflict began. The Abu Dhabi National Oil Company activated emergency protocols for offshore facilities.

## Saudi Arabia's Eastern Province

Saudi Arabia intercepted at least 36 drones aimed at its Eastern Province — home to Aramco's largest facilities including the Abqaiq processing plant handling approximately 7% of global oil supply. All drones were destroyed but the proximity to critical infrastructure alarmed energy markets globally.

## Markets React

The IRGC's formal declaration closing the Strait to US-aligned ships combined with widening Gulf strikes sent markets into freefall. Brent crude topped $110 a barrel. Wall Street recorded its worst single day since the coronavirus pandemic. The OECD kept its 2026 global growth forecast at 2.9% but warned sustained energy disruption could force significant downward revisions.

## Six Countries Now at War

Day 28 confirmed what analysts feared: the conflict has become a regional war. Six countries — Iran, Israel, Lebanon, Saudi Arabia, UAE, and Kuwait — are now either attacking or being attacked. The question is no longer whether the war will spread but how far and how fast.
