**Kyiv / Brussels — March 25, 2026**

The war with Iran is hollowing out Ukraine's lifeline. The White House formally notified NATO allies that American weapons supplies to Kyiv will be disrupted — the Middle East conflict depleting the US stockpiles from which Ukraine draws ammunition, air defence interceptors, and precision weapons.

## The Supply Chain Breaks

The notification confirmed what analysts warned for weeks: fighting two major conflicts simultaneously strains even America's vast arsenal. Warehouses supplying HIMARS rockets, Patriot interceptors, and 155mm shells to Ukraine are now being raided for Middle East operations.

Zelensky has repeatedly warned the Iran war is a "plus for Putin." The weapons disruption proves his point. Russian forces have accelerated offensive operations across multiple eastern sectors, sensing a strategic opening.

## Ukraine Running Out of Money by June

At a parliamentary meeting, Finance Minister Marchenko confirmed Ukraine's military funding lasts only until June without the first EU 90-billion-euro tranche arriving on schedule. That tranche has been delayed by procedural disputes within Brussels — meaning Ukraine faces the extraordinary prospect of being unable to fund basic military operations precisely as Russian pressure peaks.

## Russia Senses an Opening

The Institute for the Study of War assessed Russia is "gearing up for a spring offensive," emboldened by the perception that Western attention has shifted. Putin, who has argued Western support is unsustainable, may be watching Tehran with quiet satisfaction. The Iran war has done in weeks what years of Russian pressure on NATO solidarity failed to do: created a genuine crack in the Western support structure.
